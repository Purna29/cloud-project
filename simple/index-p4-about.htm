ABOUT BCCI:

INTODUCTION

The Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) is the national governing body for cricket in India.The board was formed in December 1928 as a society, registered under the Tamil Nadu Societies Registration Act. It is a consortium of state cricket associations and the state associations select their representatives who in turn elect the BCCI Chief. Its headquarters are in Wankhede Stadium, Mumbai.

HISTORY

In 1912, an all-India cricket team visited England for the first time, sponsored and captained by Maharaja of Patiala. In 1926, two representatives of the Calcutta Cricket Club travelled to London to attend meetings of the Imperial Cricket Conference, the predecessor to the current International Cricket Council. Although technically not an official representative of Indian cricket, they were allowed to attend by Lord Harris, chairman of the conference. The outcome of the meeting was the MCC's decision to send a team to India, led by Arthur Gilligan, who had captained England in The Ashes.

PRESIDENT

The current president is Sourav Ganguly who is a former captain of Indian cricket team.

ACHEIVEMENTS

IN 1983,Team India won their first world cup against West Indies under the captaincy of Kapil Dev and again in 2011 we won the second world cup after 28 years under the captaincy of Mahendra Singh Dhoni.
